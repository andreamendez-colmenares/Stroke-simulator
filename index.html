BP & glucose targets

Pre-TNK: <185/110.

Post-TNK / post-EVT, first 24 h: ≤180/105.

Pre-reperfusion BP lowering: only enough to meet eligibility threshold. Big drops compromise penumbral perfusion.

Post-successful EVT: do not push SBP <140 — Class III, harm.

Glucose: treat <60. Avoid >180. Do not tight-control 80–130.

Stroke vs mimic

Working diagnosis is stroke
Common mimics — quick reference

Hypoglycemia — check glucose first.

Todd's paralysis — witnessed seizure, positive motor onset, postictal confusion, improving deficit.

Migraine with aura — slow march over minutes, headache, known history.

Conversion / functional — non-anatomic distribution, give-way, intact reflexes.

UTI / delirium — fluctuating AMS without focal deficit.

Hyponatremia — Na <125 can produce focal signs.

Image when uncertain. Treat the differential, not the assumption.

Exam: NIHSS + LVO suspicion

LVO suspicion features

Cortical signs are more specific for LVO than total NIHSS.

Forced gaze deviation
Aphasia (dominant hemisphere)
Hemineglect / extinction
Dense hemiparesis (≥3 on one side)
LVO suspicion: —
High LVO suspicion → CTA + CTP regardless of LKW.
How LVO suspicion is derived

High = NIHSS ≥6 with any cortical sign, or ≥2 cortical signs.

Moderate = NIHSS ≥6 with no cortical signs, or NIHSS <6 with one cortical sign.

Low = NIHSS <6, no cortical signs.

Posterior caveat: NIHSS underscores posterior strokes. Vertigo, ataxia, dysarthria, decreased LOC, crossed signs warrant CTA regardless of NIHSS.

TNK Eligibility

Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg IV bolus, max 25 mg, over 5 seconds (use institutional order set for dose).

Is the deficit disabling to the patient?

Disability is patient-specific. Aphasia in a teacher, hand weakness in a surgeon, mild dysarthria in a singer — all disabling.

Disabling vs non-disabling

2026 guideline: thrombolyze any disabling deficit regardless of NIHSS. Non-disabling NIHSS 0–5 → DAPT (PRISMS).

Always ask the patient's occupation. A "mild" deficit can be career-ending in the right context.

If patient prefers DAPT after informed discussion, that is also reasonable for borderline mild stroke.

Relative contraindications (weigh risk/benefit, do not auto-exclude)

DOAC within 48 h
Recent ischemic stroke (≤3 mo)
Why relative, not absolute

DOAC <48 h: 2026 guideline reframes as relative. Anti-Xa or specific reversal (idarucizumab for dabigatran) can support TNK in select cases. Risk-benefit and attending discussion.

Recent stroke ≤3 mo: size matters. A small lacunar 6 weeks ago is not the same risk as a large MCA infarct 3 weeks ago. Larger/more recent strokes have higher hemorrhagic conversion risk with TNK.

For both: document the rationale and discuss with attending. MT alone is often the safer pathway when LVO is present.

Hemorrhage Pathway

ICH BP target (presenting SBP 150–220)

  • Lower to SBP 130 to <140. Smooth, sustained, no overshoot.
  • If SBP falls <130 — back off.
  • If presenting SBP >220, do not drop below 130, avoid large rapid swings.
  • Use IV labetalol or nicardipine drip. Avoid hydralazine boluses.
  • Mantra: fast enough, smooth enough, not too far.

SAH BP

  • Pre-securing aneurysm: SBP <160 (some <140).
  • Post-securing: more permissive; manage for vasospasm.
  • Nimodipine 60 mg PO/NG q4h × 21 d.
  • NSGY consult immediately. CTA + DSA.

Anticoagulation reversal

  • Warfarin (INR >1.4): 4F-PCC + IV vitamin K 10 mg.
  • Dabigatran: idarucizumab 5 g IV.
  • Apixaban / rivaroxaban: andexanet alfa or 4F-PCC.
  • Heparin / LMWH: protamine.
  • Antiplatelets: platelet transfusion not routine (PATCH was negative).

General care

  • HOB 30°, normothermia, normoglycemia, normonatremia.
  • Stop antithrombotics. Reverse as above.
  • Repeat NCCT at 6 h or with neuro change.
  • NeuroICU.
