Common mimics to consider

Hypoglycemia — check glucose first, every time. <60 → treat and reassess.

Post-ictal Todd's paralysis — witnessed seizure or known epilepsy with deficit that improves over hours.

UTI / delirium — fluctuating AMS, often older, no focal deficit.

Hyponatremia — Na < 125 can produce focal signs and AMS.

Migraine with aura — known history, slow march, accompanied by headache.

Conversion / functional — non-anatomic distribution, give-way weakness, Hoover's sign.

MS flare — known MS, subacute. Not a stroke.

If you cannot rule a mimic out quickly, image anyway — but keep the differential open.

LVO suspicion: —
High LVO suspicion → Order CTA + CTP regardless of LKW. CTP identifies salvageable penumbra and qualifies the patient for late-window or large-core MT pathways.
How LVO suspicion is derived

High = NIHSS ≥ 6 with any cortical sign, or ≥ 2 cortical signs.

Moderate = NIHSS ≥ 6 with no cortical signs, or NIHSS < 6 with one cortical sign.

Low = NIHSS < 6, no cortical signs.

Pre-hospital scales (RACE, FAST-ED, VAN, LAMS) all use these features. Cortical signs are more specific for LVO than total NIHSS.

Posterior circulation caveat: NIHSS systematically underscores posterior strokes. Vertigo, ataxia, dysarthria, crossed signs, or reduced consciousness with relatively low NIHSS still warrant CTA.

How window classification works (2026)

Standard window = ≤ 4.5 h from LKW. TNK Class 1 if eligible.

Extended window = 4.5–24 h with salvageable tissue on advanced imaging. MT preferred when LVO present (Class 1, 6–24 h). Extended-window TNK is Class 2b — only when MT is not feasible (TIMELESS was negative for TNK in patients who got rapid MT; HOPE/TRACE-III support TNK in selected non-MT patients).

Wake-up / unknown LKW = treat the discovery time as max bound. MRI DWI/FLAIR mismatch (positive DWI, negative FLAIR) qualifies for IVT (per WAKE-UP). Perfusion mismatch qualifies for MT.

Unknown LKW does not equal ineligible — it just routes through advanced imaging.

What CTP and MRI mismatch buy you

CTP mismatch = ischemic core + larger penumbra. Qualifies extended-window MT (DAWN/DEFUSE-3) and large-core MT (SELECT2/ANGEL-ASPECT) candidates.

DWI/FLAIR mismatch = positive DWI but negative FLAIR signal — likely < 4.5 h since stroke onset. Per WAKE-UP, qualifies for IVT in unknown-LKW patients.

The MRI mismatch is for IVT eligibility in wake-ups; the CTP mismatch is for MT eligibility in late windows. Different decisions, different imaging.

TNK Eligibility

Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg IV bolus, max 25 mg, over 5 seconds. Use your institution's order set for dose calculation.

Disabling deficit?

2026 guideline: thrombolyze any disabling deficit regardless of NIHSS. Non-disabling NIHSS 0–5 → DAPT, not TNK (PRISMS).

What counts as "disabling"?

Inability to perform ADLs or return to occupation, in the patient's own life context.

Usually disabling: any aphasia, hemianopia, neglect, hand weakness, gait ataxia, dysphagia.

Occupation-specific: mild fine-motor deficit in a surgeon, mild dysarthria in a teacher, mild hand weakness in a musician — all disabling.

Often non-disabling: isolated mild facial droop, isolated mild dysarthria with intact language, mild distal sensory loss without functional impact.

If unsure, lean toward disabling. Aphasia at NIHSS 2 still gets TNK.

Mechanical Thrombectomy Decision

Three pathways in 2026: standard MT, large-core MT, basilar.

Anterior circulation LVO — standard pathway (Class 1)

  • Anterior circulation LVO on CTA
  • 0–24 h from LKW
  • NIHSS ≥ 6
  • ASPECTS ≥ 6
  • Pre-stroke mRS ≤ 1
  • Age ≥ 18

Large core MT — expanded pathway (Class 1, new in 2026)

  • Anterior LVO with ASPECTS 3–5
  • Age < 80
  • NIHSS ≥ 6
  • 0–24 h from LKW

Based on SELECT2, ANGEL-ASPECT, TESLA. Patients previously excluded for "too much core" can now benefit.

Basilar artery occlusion (Class 1)

  • Basilar occlusion on CTA
  • NIHSS ≥ 10
  • 0–24 h from LKW

Based on BAOCHE and ATTENTION. Posterior circulation has its own window — do not apply anterior-circulation NIHSS thresholds here.

BP after EVT — 2026 key change

After successful reperfusion, do not push SBP < 140. Class III (harm).

Target < 180/105 for first 24 h. Avoid intensive lowering — the brain just got its blood back, and overcorrection causes worse outcomes.

The reflex from the ICH world ("get the BP down fast") does not apply here.

Hemorrhage Pathway

NCCT positive for hemorrhage diverts you here. Different rules entirely.

ICH BP management

  • Target SBP: 130–150 mm Hg if presenting SBP > 150. Avoid SBP < 110.
  • INTERACT-2 supports modest lowering (target <140). ATACH-II showed no benefit and more renal injury with aggressive (<140) targets — current consensus is moderate lowering.
  • Use IV labetalol or nicardipine drip. Avoid hydralazine boluses.

SAH BP management

  • Pre-securing aneurysm: SBP < 160 (some institutions < 140). Balance rebleed risk vs perfusion.
  • Post-securing: more permissive, manage for vasospasm.
  • Nimodipine 60 mg PO/NG q4h × 21 d for vasospasm prophylaxis.
  • Consult NSGY immediately. CTA + DSA for aneurysm characterization.

Anticoagulation reversal

  • Warfarin (INR > 1.4): 4-factor PCC (Kcentra) + IV vitamin K 10 mg.
  • Dabigatran: Idarucizumab 5 g IV.
  • Apixaban / rivaroxaban: Andexanet alfa (institution-dependent) or 4F-PCC.
  • Heparin / LMWH: Protamine.
  • Antiplatelets: Platelet transfusion not routinely recommended unless neurosurgical intervention planned (PATCH was negative).

General ICH/SAH care

  • HOB 30°, normothermia, normoglycemia, normonatremia.
  • Seizure prophylaxis only for lobar ICH or clinical seizures (not routine).
  • Reverse anticoagulation as above. STOP all antithrombotics.
  • Repeat NCCT at 6 h or sooner if neuro change to assess expansion.
  • Disposition: NeuroICU.
